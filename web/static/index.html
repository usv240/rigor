The math behind it

Why the verdicts can't be faked

These checks are established, peer-reviewed science. Rigor's contribution is making them work on any paper, for anyone - automatically.

p-value recomputation i

Given a test statistic and its degrees of freedom i, the true p-value is fixed. If the printed value disagrees, that's an error.

t(48) = 1.90, reported p < .001
→ recomputed p = 0.063 - not significant.

GRIM test i

The mean of N integer responses can only take certain values. Some reported means are arithmetically impossible - a fingerprint of error or fabrication.

M = 3.45, N = 10
→ impossible - nearest are 3.4 and 3.5.

Not an AI wrapper i

The model only reads; the math judges. That's why Rigor catches the errors and leaves correct results alone - no crying wolf on honest science.
